Stephen Curry

The Man, The Myth, The Legend

Stephen Curry's Timeline of Greatness

1988 - Born in Akron, Ohio, but raised in Charlotte, North Carolina. Son of former NBA player Dell Curry.

2006 - Enrolls at Davidson College, quickly gaining attention for his incredible shooting ability.

2008 - Leads Davidson to the Elite Eight in the NCAA Tournament, shocking the basketball world with his deep threes.

2009 - Drafted 7th overall by the Golden State Warriors in the NBA Draft.

2013 - Sets the NBA single-season record for most three-pointers made (272), solidifying himself as one of the best shooters.

2015 - Wins his first NBA Championship with the Warriors and earns his first MVP award.

2016 - Becomes the first-ever unanimous MVP, leading the Warriors to a 73-9 record, the best regular-season record in NBA history.

2017 - Wins his second NBA Championship as part of the Warriors' superteam with Kevin Durant.

2018 - Secures back-to-back titles, proving his dominance.

2021 - Breaks the NBA all-time record for most three-pointers made, surpassing Ray Allen.

2022 - Leads the Warriors to another NBA Championship, winning Finals MVP for the first time.

Present - Continues to dominate the league, inspiring a new generation of players with his limitless shooting range and leadership.

His peak was supposed to be past him. His best days were supposed to be yesteryear. But the id in Curry resisted that notion. He imposed his will on time itself, warding off its intent to decay. So he could orchestrate the greatest performance of his life. So he could have the season no one imagined he could have. So he could flex.
